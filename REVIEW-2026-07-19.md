## Executive summary

- **Overall health: sound and close to press-ready.** The review surfaced no high-severity defects — 6 medium items and roughly thirty low ones, the lows dominated by cosmetic spelling/casing consistency. The argument, structure, and citation discipline are strong; what remains is a cleanup pass, not a rewrite.
- **One genuine error to fix before circulation:** the French-corpus figure caption in `07-outlook.tex:64/66` contradicts itself — "only 19.7% of projects carry a licence — three-quarters have none," when 19.7% carrying implies **four-fifths** have none (and the body text already says "about a fifth").
- **Citation hygiene has a few holes in an otherwise scrupulously cited text.** Three hard, checkable numbers in `01-why.tex:86`, the URL-specification quotation in `03-foundations.tex:49`, and the ~90% GitHub-no-licence figure in `05-ardc.tex:231` each stand without an `\autocite`, uniquely among their neighbours.
- **Completeness gap in the policy appendix:** `E-policy.tex:15` promises "every policy instrument surfaced in these notes," yet the ANR funding guideline — which Ch.7 gives a full paragraph and figure — has no row. A French researcher will look for exactly that funder line.
- **biblatex-software showcase verdict — NOT fully delivered.** The note names four entry types, but the reader sees only **three** render (`@software`, `@softwareversion`, `@codefragment`). `@softwaremodule` — the very variant the Ch.5 hands-on lab tells students to "produce and compare" (`05-ardc.tex:402`) — is never shown, rendered, or placed in `references.bib` anywhere, so students have no model for the one type the exercise requires.
- **Uncited-entry question:** the `@software{softwareheritage}` and `@software{biblatexsoftware}` entries exist in `references.bib` but are cited zero times (absent from `main.bbl`), so neither ever prints in References. The note about citing software as first-class does not yet dogfood its own advice — two `\autocite` calls close both the dead entries and the credibility gap.
- **Spelling house-style is unsettled, and reviewers disagree on the target.** Findings pull in opposite directions (`artifact` vs `artefact`; `organisation` vs `organization`; `recognised` vs `recognized`). This is a single editorial decision — pick one convention per word-family and apply it document-wide — that clears an entire cluster of low findings at once.
- **The rest is voice and macro polish:** self-echoes ("uncomfortable" ×3, "We now have" across the Ch.4/5 seam, the "menu" metaphor twice) and small-caps flicker (`\textsc{swhid}`/`ardc`/`dag` used inconsistently) — all low, all quick.
